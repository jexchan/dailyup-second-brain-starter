# ACTOR Framework Reference

This reference distills the Sandeep Swadia video subtitle `how-to-become-dangerously-self-educated-with-ai.srt` into reusable skill guidance.

## Core Diagnosis

Common reading traps:

- **Learning-style label trap**: people over-identify with "I am a visual/auditory/doing learner" and set an unnecessary ceiling.
- **Fluency illusion**: clear explanation feels like clear understanding until the reader must explain the mechanism step by step.
- **Highlighter trap**: marking sentences feels like memory.
- **Summary trap**: polished notes are created and never used.
- **Completion trap**: the book is finished, but nothing is formed inside the reader.
- **AI shortcut trap**: AI summaries create the feeling of having read without the struggle required to explain, remember, and apply.

## ACTOR Moves

### Aim

Best readers read with a mission. Before reading, write:

```text
I am reading this because I need to XYZ.
```

AI role: framer. It helps create purposeful questions before the material starts deciding what matters.

### Compress

Read to carry less, not collect more. Use the tree model:

- Root: personal/project reason for reading.
- Trunk: central load-bearing idea.
- Branches: major arguments or chapters.
- Leaves: examples, quotes, stories, details.

AI role: interpreter. Ask it to check your candidate trunk: what is missing, misunderstood, or overstated?

### Test

Serious readers do not read only to agree. They use disagreement to investigate both the author and themselves.

Questions:

- Why did this bother me?
- What belief am I protecting?
- Where is the author right?
- Where is the author wrong?
- What would I need to believe to argue the opposite?
- Where would this advice fail?

AI role: sparring partner.

### Own

You own an idea when you can retrieve it, explain it in your own words, connect it to real life, and teach it.

Practices:

- Look away and recall.
- Explain in one or two paragraphs.
- Connect to a meeting, mistake, company, person, conversation, or old belief.
- Teach it to someone, even if that someone is AI.

AI role: coach. It helps check whether the explanation hits the right notes.

### Run

Reading should interrupt behavior. Convert the idea into:

- one decision
- one rule
- one checklist
- one experiment

AI role: action companion.

## Design Implication for the Skill

The assistant should resist staying at the level of summary. Every substantial response should move at least one step toward user-owned understanding or real-world action.
