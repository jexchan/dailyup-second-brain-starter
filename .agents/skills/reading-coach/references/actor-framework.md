# ACTOR Source and Learning Traps

This reference distills the Sandeep Swadia video subtitle
`how-to-become-dangerously-self-educated-with-ai.srt`.

## Core Diagnosis

Common reading traps:

- **Learning-style label trap**: people over-identify with "I am a visual/auditory/doing learner" and set an unnecessary ceiling.
- **Fluency illusion**: clear explanation feels like clear understanding until the reader must explain the mechanism step by step.
- **Highlighter trap**: marking sentences feels like memory.
- **Summary trap**: polished notes are created and never used.
- **Completion trap**: the book is finished, but nothing is formed inside the reader.
- **AI shortcut trap**: AI summaries create the feeling of having read without the struggle required to explain, remember, and apply.

## AI Role Map

- **Aim — framer**: sharpen the mission and reading questions.
- **Compress — interpreter**: check the user's candidate structure.
- **Test — sparring partner**: examine the author, reader, and context.
- **Own — coach**: test retrieval and repair explanation gaps.
- **Run — action companion**: shape a small, verifiable application.
